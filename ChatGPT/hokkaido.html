Getting to Hokkaido

There are several ways to reach Hokkaido, including by plane, train, or ferry. The main airport in Hokkaido is New Chitose Airport, which is located just outside of Sapporo. From there, you can take a train, bus, or taxi to your destination. If you're coming from Honshu, the main island of Japan, you can also take the ferry from several ports, including Tokyo and Osaka.

Where to Stay

Hokkaido has a wide range of accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxurious resorts. Some of the most popular destinations for travelers include Sapporo, Niseko, and Otaru. No matter where you stay, be sure to book your accommodation in advance, as Hokkaido is a popular tourist destination, and hotels can fill up quickly during peak season.

What to Do

There is no shortage of things to do in Hokkaido. Some of the top activities include:

  • Hiking: Hokkaido is home to some of Japan's most beautiful and scenic hiking trails. Whether you're a seasoned hiker or just looking for a leisurely stroll, you'll find something to suit your needs.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Hokkaido is famous for its world-class ski resorts, including Niseko and Rusutsu. Whether you're an experienced skier or just starting out, you'll find plenty of runs to challenge and excite you.
  • Seafood: Hokkaido is renowned for its delicious seafood, including crab, scallops, and salmon. Be sure to try some of the local specialties, like sushi and sashimi, when you're here.
  • Onsen: An onsen is a traditional Japanese hot spring, and there are many to be found throughout Hokkaido. Soaking in an onsen is the perfect way to relax after a day of adventure.
